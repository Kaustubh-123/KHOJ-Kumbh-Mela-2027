## Project Description
KHOJ is a privacy-first, edge-AI triage and decentralized missing persons framework designed for extreme-density environments. Winning the Claude Impact Lab Hackathon validated our core hypothesis: privacy-first edge AI can solve extreme-density crisis management. However, a hackathon is just a Proof of Concept. We are applying to the Claude Open Source Program because we need Anthropic's support, compute, and mentorship to harden this infrastructure for our target deployment at the 2027 Nashik Kumbh Mela (100M+ attendees). 

As maintainers, we take a highly disciplined approach to stability. You will notice we have split our ecosystem into two repositories:
1. KHOJ-Kumbh-Mela-2027 (The repository submitted here): This is our v1.0 stable release, featuring our flawless, Edge-ready Next.js Kiosk UI. 
2. Claude-Impact-Lab-Khoj-All (Linked in our Roadmap): This is our active-development v2 monorepo housing our experimental FastAPI microservices, Telegram bots, and decentralized ML matching engine. 

Our ultimate goal over the next 12 months is to merge these experimental microservices into the stable core, leveraging Claude 3.5 Sonnet's massive context window to ingest unstructured, panic-driven text reports from SMS and Telegram. We want to transition KHOJ from a successful prototype into a free, white-label framework for global disaster relief agencies.
